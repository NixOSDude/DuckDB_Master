// SOVEREIGN SQL ENGINE

Capabilities your current stack
doesn't have.

Alongside raw speed, I built a custom SQL extension layer that solves problems standard DuckDB — and every other tool — cannot answer out of the box.

Automatic Data Normalization

Your CRM, ERP, or product catalog stores data as key-value rows — one row per attribute per entity. Standard SQL requires you to hard-code every field name. My engine discovers the structure dynamically and rebuilds it as a clean, queryable wide table automatically. No manual pivot logic. No brittle CASE statements. Works on any data shape.

Graph & Hierarchy Traversal

Who reports to whom, three levels deep? Which suppliers feed into this part? What accounts are connected to this flagged transaction? Standard SQL cannot walk relationships without brittle recursive queries. My engine traverses any directed graph in a single SQL call — org charts, supply chains, category trees, dependency graphs — returning every node, its depth, and the exact path to reach it.

Overnight Change Detection

Compare any two snapshots of your data and get a precise, row-level breakdown of what was added, removed, or modified — with the full before and after state as JSON. Automated data quality monitoring, pipeline validation, slowly changing dimensions, audit trails. One SQL call replaces hundreds of lines of FULL OUTER JOIN logic. Runs nightly without supervision.

Column-Level Encryption in SQL
Sensitive fields — email, phone, pricing, IP — encrypted individually using ML-KEM-768 + AES-256-GCM before they enter any pipeline. The rest of the table is fully queryable. Only the keyholder can decrypt. A stolen database export reveals nothing. No other fractional engineer offers this.
Cryptographically Signed Deliveries
Every report or dataset I deliver can be signed with a post-quantum digital signature. Your team verifies the signature against my public key to confirm the data is exactly what I produced — unmodified, authenticated, tamper-evident. Chain of custody, in SQL.
Future-Proof Against Quantum Attacks
RSA and ECC encryption — what protects most data today — will be broken by quantum computers. NIST finalized the post-quantum standards in 2024. My encryption stack uses those standards now. Data encrypted today stays safe when quantum arrives.
